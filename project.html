01 — Overview

Making personal finance feel human

Clari is a fintech startup building an AI-powered personal finance app for the Mexican market. When they came to me, their app had a 34% onboarding dropout rate and users were calling the dashboard "a spreadsheet that moves".

The brief was clear: redesign the product from onboarding to core flows, introduce an AI insights layer, and establish a design system the team could build on for the next 2 years.

"We want people to feel like they have a smart financial friend in their pocket — not another banking app."

— CEO, Clari

02 — Research

15 interviews. 3 brutal truths.

I ran 15 moderated user interviews with Mexicans between 23 and 38 who actively managed their finances on mobile. Combined with session recordings and a competitive audit of 6 apps, three patterns emerged immediately.

01

Trust is the barrier. Users hesitated to link bank accounts because they didn't understand what data was being accessed or why.

02

Insights feel like lectures. The existing AI output was presented as a list of warnings. People felt judged, not helped.

03

Goals are aspirational, not transactional. Users wanted to see progress toward things they care about — not raw numbers.

Affinity Map

03 — Solution

A financial companion, not a dashboard

The redesign centered on three shifts: from data display to actionable story, from warnings to encouragement, and from raw metrics to goal-driven progress.

The onboarding flow was rebuilt around progressive trust — users could explore the app before linking accounts, and each permission request was accompanied by a plain-language explanation of exactly what it enabled.

The AI layer, powered by Claude, surfaced insights as conversational nudges at the moment they were most useful — after a purchase, before a bill, or when savings milestones were close.
